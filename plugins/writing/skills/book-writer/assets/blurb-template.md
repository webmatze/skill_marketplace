# Blurb — [Book title]

> Back-cover / Amazon book description. Aim for 150–200 words. Anything past the first ~200 characters gets cut behind a "Read more" on Amazon mobile, so the hook has to land fast.

## Headline / hook (1 line)

[A question, a stark statement, or a single-line situation that hints at the wrongness in the protagonist's world.]

## Body — fiction structure

**Stakes paragraph** (3–5 sentences):
[Who the protagonist is. What they want. What stands in the way. Concrete, not abstract.]

**Twist or escalation** (1–2 sentences):
[The moment the reader realises this book has more than the obvious plot. The buried body, the secret, the deadline, the second betrayal.]

**Tagline** (1 line, often italicised):
[The emotional pitch. "Some doors should stay locked." "She didn't want a second chance. She wanted revenge."]

## Body — non-fiction structure

**Pain hook** (1–2 sentences):
[The reader's specific problem. Concrete enough that the reader thinks "yes, that's me."]

**Promise** (2–3 sentences):
[What the book delivers that other books don't. The angle, the framework, the difference.]

**Credibility** (1–2 sentences):
[Why this book / this author. Optional if the author has no relevant credentials beyond writing the book — in that case, replace with a "what you'll learn" beat.]

**Outcome** (1–2 sentences):
[Concrete things the reader will be able to do or believe by the end.]

**Call to action** (1 line):
["If you've ever [pain], this book is your [solution]." or similar.]

## Final blurb (paste-ready)

[The actual blurb, ~150–200 words, ready to paste into KDP. No section headers; just the prose.]

---

## Checklist before finalising

- [ ] First sentence hooks within the first 100 characters
- [ ] Protagonist named (fiction) or reader's pain named (non-fiction) within the first paragraph
- [ ] At least one specific concrete detail (not just genre-generic mood)
- [ ] No spoilers past the inciting incident
- [ ] No "this book will...", "in this book you'll learn..." (passive, blurby)
- [ ] No exclamation marks (cheapens almost any blurb; rare exceptions for comedy)
- [ ] Reads aloud cleanly
- [ ] Length: 150–200 words
